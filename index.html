Technology-Mediated Communication

What is Tech-Mediated Communication?

Technology-Mediated Communication (TMC) can be thought of as any form of communication that is done through or with the help of technology. Some examples of tech-mediated communication include texting, instant messaging, emailing, forums, and chatrooms.

Lifespan U18

What does Lifespan U18 Mean?

Lifespan U18 refers to audiences that are under 18 years old. For the purposes of the ideas covered in this site, Lifespan U18 will mainly refer to ~13 to 17 year olds.

When Do Children Get Their Technology?

The technology people use to aid in their communication with others in their everyday lives has been changing rapidly each year. Not only that, but the ability to obtain the technology has improved as well. With that, the age in which children first get technology is getting younger and younger. In 2016, the average age for a child to get their first cell phone was about 10 years old, and about 50% of kids have their first social media accounts by age 12. [1]

Technology Has Changed

Education

Technology has changed so many aspects of education. The way children receive their learning, how they do their school work, and how they interact with other classmates and their own teachers are just a few things that have changed dramatically because of tech-mediated communication.

Social Media
