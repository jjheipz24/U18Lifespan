The Evolution of Educational Technology

Technology in the classroom is not a new concept. Before the year 2000, many classrooms had access to radios (1920), overhead projectors (1930), ballpoint pens (1940), headphones (1950), videotapes (1951), photocopiers (1959), handheld calculators and the Scantron System(1972).[6] After 2000, computers and laptops were introduced, which was a major turning point in educational technology. By 2009, 97% of classrooms had at least one computern and 93% of classrooms had internet access.[6] Since then, schools purchased over 23 million devices for classroom use, including iPads and Chromebooks, in just 2013 alone.[7]
